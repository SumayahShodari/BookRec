Here's some of Jana's books Recommendations :


1) THE ORIGIN

it is part of the Robert Langdon series, following the adventures of Harvard symbologist Robert Langdon. In "Origin," Langdon finds himself embroiled in a mystery surrounding a groundbreaking discovery that could answer humanity's oldest and most profound questions regarding its origin and future. The novel delves into themes of science, religion, and the potential impact of technological advancements on society. Like Dan Brown's other novels, "Origin" is known for its intricate plot, historical references, and fast-paced storytelling.



2) The Story of Philosophy

The book serves as an accessible introduction to the history of philosophy. Will Durant, a Pulitzer Prize-winning historian, presents a comprehensive overview of Western philosophical thought from ancient times to the early 20th century. In "The Story of Philosophy," Durant provides biographical sketches of prominent philosophers and explains their key ideas and contributions to the field of philosophy. The book aims to make philosophy understandable and engaging to a wide audience, and it has remained popular for its lucid writing style and insightful analysis.



3) 21 Lessons for the 21st Century

Yes, "21 Lessons for the 21st Century" is a book written by Yuval Noah Harari, an Israeli historian and author known for his insightful commentary on the past, present, and future of humanity. Published in 2018, the book explores various pressing issues and challenges facing society in the modern era.In "21 Lessons for the 21st Century," Harari offers readers a thought-provoking examination of topics such as technology, politics, nationalism, religion, and existential threats like climate change and artificial intelligence. Through twenty-one essays or lessons, he addresses complex questions and provides insights into how individuals and societies can navigate the uncertainties of the contemporary world. The book aims to stimulate critical thinking and promote a deeper understanding of the forces shaping our lives in the 21st century. Harari draws on his expertise in history, philosophy, and science to offer readers new perspectives on the challenges and opportunities of our time. .
